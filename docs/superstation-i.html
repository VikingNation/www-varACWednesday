VarAC SUPERSTATION I Contest set for January 14th - 28th, 2026

The global VarAC community is excited for the initial running of the SUPERSTATION game. Unlike most amateur radio contests, SUPERSTATION encourages long form QSOs of at least ten minutes over an extended period of time. The game is unique in that it combines having QSOs with a card & dice game as well as the distance of the longest QSO during the contest period.

The high scorer will receive a beautiful full color plaque. The contest’s official sponsor is QRP Labs.

VarAC provides error free keyboard to keyboard communication. Built upon the VARA HF modem, QSOs of -20dB can be achieved. VarAC and SUPERSTATION are free applications. VARA HF is free as well although higher performance can be unlocked with a $75.00 license.

RULES

  1. The SUPERSTATION I contest runs from 0000Z January 14 through 2359Z January 28, 2026.
  2. Users must configure VarAC and SUPERSTATION per documentation.
  3. Users must RESTART (Game → Restart) SUPERSTATION before the first contest QSO or <ROLL>.
  4. Users must also set the Start and End Dates (File → Setup).
  5. Users must configure their grid square in SUPERSTATION (File → Setup).
  6. All users must provide a valid working email address when generating the Cabrillo log.
  7. To have a score greater than zero, you must play the SUPERSTATION dice and card game until you have built-out your virtual station with antenna, radio, computer, and amplifier cards.
  8. All valid SUPERSTATION QSOs made during the contest period count toward your final score. You may, therefore, make QSOs while you are building out your virtual station.
  9. You can only play the SUPERSTATION dice and card game three times per day. The maximum you can score is 256 which is the SUPERSTATION.
  10. To be counted as a valid SUPERSTATION QSO, it must be greater than 10 minutes and each QSO participant must have at least four lines of keyboard-to-keyboard typed text considered human-to-human.
  11. Human-to-machine lines (signal reports, tags, VMAILs, AI chat lines, VarAC system messages) do not count toward the four human-to-human lines.
  12. You must log QSOs in the VarAC application with the grid square of your QSO participant for distance multipliers.
  13. SUPERSTATION computes the total score as: SCORE = (Virtual Station value 0–256) × (# of QSOs ≥10 minutes) × (Maximum distance of a single human-to-human contact).
  14. Logs must be submitted by 2359Z January 30, 2026 via SUPERSTATION website.
  15. Unsportsmanlike conduct (robot QSOs, falsified grid squares, gaming the system) will result in disqualification.
  16. Contest participants shall not modify scoring software or Cabrillo log outputs.
  17. Organizers may require software updates during the contest; logs must be generated with the required version.
  18. The top 3 scorers will be requested to provide Debug Logs for verification.
  19. Final results are binding once log checking is complete; no further corrections or inquiries will be accepted.
