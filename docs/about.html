What is VarAC Wednesday?

VarAC Wednesday is a weekly amateur radio digital net where users check-in by sending a message via a station providing EMAIL gateway service.

What is the purpose of VarAC Wednesday?

VarAC Wednesday promotes awareness and practical use of VarAC’s messaging capabilities, including peer-to-peer VMAIL, parked messages, and email gateway delivery. The weekly net enhances operator proficiency through consistent engagement and training.

Why should I participate weekly?

Regular participation builds familiarity with VarAC’s features, improves technical readiness, and encourages routine testing of equipment and infrastructure—including email gateway nodes.

We recognize participants through awards / certificates as well as posts on social media to highlight operators who participate.

Is VarAC Wednesday associated with emergency communications?

While not formally affiliated with emergency communication groups, the skills developed through VarAC Wednesday directly support groups providing emergency communications and the agencies they serve.

What is the relationship to Winlink Wednesday?

VarAC Wednesday has no connection or relationship with the original Winlink Wednesday or any other organization who conduct Winlink testing. VarAC Wednesday uses the same message format as Winlink Wednesday. We encourage operators to participate in both weekly digital nets.

How do I compose the check-in message?

Open VarAC and click the VMAIL button. Click the "Send as email" check-box. Type "varacwednesday@gmail.com" in the "To" line. Add a comma and type an email address to receive a copy. The copy is used to verify your VMAIL was delivered from the VarAC gateway to the Internet. Next, type a reply to address. Fill out the subject line and message portion based upon what is required for the given check-in week.

What do I use as the subject and message body?

Subject: VarAC Wednesday Check-In
Message body: call sign, first name, city or town, county (if applicable), country or state (HF or VHF, etc.)

EXAMPLE 1 Message body:

WA8YNQ, Mark, Cincinnati, Hamilton, OH (HF)

EXAMPLE 2 Message body:

ZS1OSS, Daniel, Cape Town, South Africa (VHF)

How do I send a copy to my CALLSIGN@WINLINK.ORG email address?

Winlink implements a filter for inbound email messages (Reference: Winlink HELP website) Add the text "//WL2K R/" at the start of your Subject line to allow the message to be delivered to your CALLSIGN@WINLINK.ORG inbox. The "//WL2K" informs WINLINK mail servers to by-pass the anti-spam ACCEPTLIST and routes the message. The "R" letter indicates routine delivery. Including this tag will not affect our software's ability to process your check-in. For example

Subject: //WL2K R/VarAC Wednesday Check-In

Why should I include my personal email in the To line?

Including your email address in the "To" line will send you a copy of the message. If you received a copy of your check-in message then most likely we received a copy (provided our email address was spelled correctly). If you did not receive a copy then there might be an issue at the email gateway station. We recommend sending a check-in using another email gateway to make sure your check-in messages is received.

Why might an email gateway fail to deliver email messages?

There are several reasons why an email might fail to deliver your check-in message (operator of email gateway might have auto delivery turned off, the password or SMTP settings at gateway are incorrect, the internet connection at gateway might be down, the operator has enabled a filter to limit who is allowed to send an email through their station, etc.) We recommended you reach out to the operator of the gateway station to report any email delivery issues. We recommend looking up the operator's callsign on QRZ.com to obtain their email address. If an email is not found then you can leave a VMAIL directed to the call sign of the operator of the gateway letting them know there might be an issue at their station.

First week of month optional message

On the first Wednesday of the month a weather report is requested, but not required. The emphasis is on brevity and communicating the information rather than applying a strict format. Please include your weather report on the line below your check-in.

EXAMPLE 1: At 0934 local time, it is clear, sunny and 51°F. Winds are out of the NE at 10 mph.

To: varacwednesday@gmail.com
Subject: VarAC Wednesday Check-In
Message body:
KB0US, Twin Falls, ID (HF)
0934L, Clear, Sunny, 10mph NE, 51F

EXAMPLE 2: At 1317 UTC, it is overcast with light rain, 10°C, wind from the SW at 5 km/h

To: varacwednesday@gmail.com
Subject: VarAC Wednesday Check-In
Message body:
VE3XYZ, John, Toronto, Canada
1317Z, overcast, light rain, 5 km/h SW,10C


Third week of month optional message

On the third Wednesday of the month an ICS-213 report is requested, but not required.

Incident Command System (ICS) forms were developed by FEMA (the U.S. Federal Emergency Management Agency) and are designed to facilitate structured communication during disaster response, exercises, or other coordinated efforts. The most commonly used ICS form in Winlink is the ICS-213, a general message form for transmitting formal messages between agencies or operators. In Winklink Express, these are HTML forms which are filled out in a browser that is then transferred to a Winlink Express email as text for sending. We don't have the HTML form, BUT we do have the text form as a TEMPLATE in the SEND VMAIL dialog box . but only in EMCOMM mode. For those wishing to practice sending ICS forms using VarAC, VarAC Wednesday gives you an opportunity to do so.

Below is an example of the text on filling on an ICS-213 to send as a VMAIL in VarAC

  • 1. INCIDENT NAME: VarAC Wednesday

  • 2. TO/Position: KB0US, Net Control
  • 3. FROM/Position: WA8YNQ, Participant
  • 4. SUBJECT: VarAC Wednesday Check-in
  • 5. DATE: 2025-07-16
  • 6. TIME: 08:00L
  • 7. MESSAGE:
    WA8YNQ, Mark, Cincinnati, Ohio (HF)
    39° 14.21421 N 84° 22.88827 W
  • 8. APPROVED BY
  • > Name / Position: Mark Rosneck, Net Participant

  • 9. REPLY:

  • 10. REPLIED BY
  • > Name/Position:
  • > Date/Time:

There are actually a number of ways to produce the ICS-213 message including

  • Fill out the form in EmComm mode, change the subject to "VarAC Wednesday Check-in", press send to put the message in the Outbox, and connect to a beacon in EmComm mode to experience the entire emergency message system.
  • Fill out the form in EmComm mode, press send to put the message in the Outbox, change the mode to Advanced, and connect to a beacon station. This avoids any concerns of sending traffic in EmComm mode which, while it is a drill, is certainly not emergency traffic.i
  • If you are also participating in Winlink Wednesday, you could fill in the form on Winlink Express and copy it into a regular VarAC email.
  • You could create a prefilled form as a canned message (now available in V11.2.0 in the VMAIL dialog box) and then manually change the time and date. An ICS-213 canned message generator comming soon!

Sending your message through an Email Gateway

You will need to send your check-in through a VarAC station that provides email gateway service.

  • Identify a clear slot to pass your email to a beacon. Using the Slot Sniffer in the CALL CQ menu is probably easiest although some will just look at their panadapter.
  • Return to calling frequency.
  • Connect to a beacon displaying the Email Gateway icon.
  • Send a QSY request to the beacon for the slot you identified as open.
  • You and the beacon both automatically move to the open slot.
  • The email is automatically transferred.
  • Disconnect when the email is transferred.

In fact, if both you and the beacon are set up correctly, you should not have to touch the keyboard after sending the QSY request; everything should continue automatically up until you need to click DISCONNECT or send a to the beacon.

How do you determine my location?

We use the following steps to determine your location. If a step is satisfied we stop and use that location information to plot on the check-in map:

  • Use GPS location in the check-in message
  • Use participant's callsign to look up their location information on QRZ.com
    • *** FUTURE PLANS *** Use GPS coordinates from the participant's QRZ.com profile
    • Use Grid square from the participant's QRZ.com profile and convert to GPS coordiante

Why is my location on the map incorrect?

Please check out our vido, Why am I missing from the check-in map?

If you did not provide your GPS location in the check-in, we pull your location from QRZ.com. Please check your profile on QRZ and update as needed.

If you did provide your GPS location in the check-in please double check what you provided and where your are located. If after that things still look to be in error please Contact Us and ask us to check into the issue.
